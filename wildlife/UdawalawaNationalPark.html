Udawalawa National Park

Udawalawe National Park is a world-renowned wildlife reserve in Sri Lanka, famous for its large elephant population, open grassland scenery, rich birdlife, and exciting safari experiences.

History & Majesty

Udawalawe National Park is one of Sri Lanka's most important wildlife reserves. It is located on the boundary of the Sabaragamuwa and Uva Provinces, surrounding the Udawalawe Reservoir. The park was established in 1972 to protect wildlife and provide a sanctuary for animals displaced by the construction of the reservoir.

Udawalawe can be visited throughout the year. However, the dry season from May to September is often considered ideal because animals gather around water sources.

Quick Facts

  • Sabaragamuwa and Uva Provinces
  • May to September
  • Best Time: Morning Safari: 6:00 AM – 10:00/11:00 AM
    Evening Safari: 2:00 PM – 6:00 PM
  • Entrance Fee Applies

What to Bring

  • Bottled Water
  • Sun Hat & Sunscreen
  • Comfortable Walking Shoes
