UNESCO World Heritage

Sinharaja Rain Forest

Sinharaja Forest Reserve is Sri Lanka’s last viable area of primary tropical rainforest and one of the country's most valuable biodiversity hotspots.

History & Majesty

Declared a Forest Reserve in 1875 and later designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1988, Sinharaja plays a crucial role in conserving Sri Lanka’s unique rainforest ecosystem. The reserve protects many species found nowhere else in the world and serves as an important center for scientific research and environmental conservation.

The forest reserve is located in the south-western region of Sri Lanka, spanning the districts of Ratnapura, Galle, and Matara. Covering approximately 11,000 hectares, Sinharaja forms part of Sri Lanka’s wet zone and receives abundant rainfall throughout the year.
