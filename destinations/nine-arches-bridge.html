Colonial Engineering

Nine Arches Bridge

A masterpiece of railway construction set deep in the tea plantations of Demodara.

Bridge in the Sky

The Nine Arches Bridge in Demodara is one of the most iconic pieces of railway architecture in Sri Lanka. Hidden away in the lush jungle and tea fields near the mountain town of Ella, it presents a picture-perfect scene straight out of a storybook.

Commissioned under the British rule, the bridge was built entirely out of brick, rock, and cement without a single piece of steel. According to local lore, when World War I broke out, the steel assigned for the site was reallocated to Britain's war effort. As a result, local builders took over and finished the bridge using stone bricks.

Today, visitors gather around the bridge to watch the famous blue trains snake their way across the sweeping curves, making for stunning photography and an unforgettable memory of the Sri Lankan highlands.
